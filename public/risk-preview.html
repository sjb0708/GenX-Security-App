Evacuation Routes Share Single Egress Street
Both the primary route (Gates A/B/C → Main St) and secondary route (Stage exits → Robson St) converge within 150ft of each other. In a mass evacuation scenario this creates a critical bottleneck for 1,884 attendees and may violate NFPA 101 separation requirements.
Identify a third evacuation corridor — consider the Centennial Way side entrance as an independent route. Coordinate with Mesa Fire for pre-event walk-through sign-off.
⛔
LEO Ratio Below Recommended Threshold
4 LEOs assigned for a 1,884-capacity indoor event. Industry standard for ticketed entertainment events is 1 LEO per 250–300 attendees, suggesting a minimum of 6–8 officers. Current staffing provides approximately 1:471 coverage.
Request 2–4 additional off-duty officers through Mesa PD or a licensed security contractor. Prioritize placement at main entry gates and backstage access points.
⛔
No Medical Coverage Noted for VIP Mezzanine Level
First aid stations are documented at Sections 101, 114, and Backstage Right — all on the main floor level. The VIP Lounge (Mezzanine) hosts 50 guests during Meet & Greet with no dedicated medical access point. AED locations do not include the mezzanine level.
Station one EMT or trained first responder at the mezzanine level during VIP/M&G hours (19:00–19:50). Relocate or add one AED unit to the mezzanine level access corridor.

30-Minute Ingress Window Is Aggressive for Venue Capacity
Doors open at 18:30, show starts at 20:00 — providing a 90-minute ingress window. However with 3 gates, magnetometer screening, and bag check, maximum throughput is approximately 400–500 guests per gate per hour. Crowd density at gates between 18:30–19:15 may be significant.
Consider opening VIP gate (Gate C) at 17:30 as planned, and pre-position 2 additional ushers at the primary gate choke points for the first 45 minutes of general admission.
⚠️
Talent Arrival and Load-In Overlap Window
Production load-in begins at 08:00 and talent arrives at 16:00 via the same Stage Door corridor. This creates a 60-minute overlap where uncleared production personnel may still be in backstage corridors during talent movement.
Implement a 15:30 "backstage clear" — all non-credentialed production personnel must clear Stage Door corridor by 15:45 ahead of talent motorcade arrival at 16:00.
⚠️
Gift Policy Lacks Enforcement Mechanism
Brief states no food gifts accepted and all gifts must be inspected, but no designated gift inspection station or documented chain-of-custody procedure is specified. In a 50-person M&G environment this creates a practical enforcement gap.
Designate one security staff member exclusively as gift inspector during M&G. Establish a physical inspection table at VIP Lounge entrance. Log all approved gifts before delivery to production office.
⚠️
Load-Out Ends at 02:00 — Reduced Overnight Staffing Risk
Load-out is documented to complete by 02:00 AM with security presence maintained. No specific staffing count is assigned to overnight load-out operations. Late-night dock operations present elevated vulnerability with reduced visibility and minimal public presence.
Assign minimum 2 security staff to dock area through load-out completion. Confirm dock lighting is operational and coordinate with venue on after-hours security camera monitoring.
